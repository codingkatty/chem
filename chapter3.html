Relative Atomic Mass and Relative Molecular Mass

The average mass of an atom of the element compared to 1/12 of the mass of one carbon-12 (C-12) atom. Why carbon-12? Because its solid at room temperature (handled easily), combines easily with other elements and found in most substances.

The relative molecular mass of a molecule can be calculated by adding up the relative atomic masses of all the atoms that form the molecule.

Relative Formula Mass, RFM

The relative mass of an ionic substance is called the relative formula mass, RFM. The relative formula mass is calculated by summing up the relative atomic masses of all the atoms shown in the formula of the ionic substance.

Chemical Formulae

Chemical formula is a representation of a chemical substance using alphabets to represent the atoms and subscript numbers to show the number of each type of atoms found in the elementary entities of the substance. Here are some examples.

Name of Compound Chemical Formula Number of Each Element in the Compound
Helium He One helium atom
Oxygen O2 2 oxygen atoms
Water H2O 2 hydrogen atoms and 1 oxygen atom
Ammonia NH3 1 nitrogen atom and 3 hydrogen atoms
Sulphuric acid H2SO4 2 hydrogen atoms, 1 sulphur atom, and 4 oxygen atoms

How is it formed?

To form a chemical formula, balance the charges of positive (cation) and negative (anion) ions so the total charge is neutral. First, identify the charge of each ion. Then, find the least common multiple (LCM) of their charges. The LCM tells us how many of each ion are needed to cancel out the charges. Assign subscripts based on this number, ensuring the total positive and negative charges equal zero. For example, a +2 cation and -3 anion will need 3 cations and 2 anions, giving you a neutral compound.

Empirical formula

The chemical formula that shows the simplest ratio of the number of atoms of each element in a compound. Here is an example of how to find it.

Element C H
Mass (g) 96 18
Number of Moles 96 / 12 = 8 18 / 1 = 18
Mole Ratio 8 18
Simplest Mol Ratio 8 / 2 = 4 18 / 2 = 9

Empirical formula: C4H9
